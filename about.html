Common Art Styles

The following are some common art styles that we like to get our art students familiar with.

ABSTRACT STYLE
Art that illustrates anything, but not in the truest of forms, can be classified as abstract art. Every object on the canvas is represented as either colors and or shapes. A simple shape on the painting can do many wonders. The artist might augment the shape with some simple exaggerations. Sometimes, the shape is scaled down to produce the effect the artist wants on the canvas.

POP ART
Pop art is an art movement that emerged in the United Kingdom and the United States during the mid- to late-1950s.The movement presented a challenge to traditions of fine art by including imagery from popular and mass culture, such as advertising, comic books and mundane mass-produced objects.
